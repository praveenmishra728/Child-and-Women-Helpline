Safety Tips & Guidelines

Practical, expert-backed safety tips for women, children, and the elderly across different situations.

📱

Share Live Location

Always share your live location with a trusted contact when traveling alone, especially at night. Use Google Maps or WhatsApp live location.

🚨

Save Emergency Contacts

Save 112 (Police), 181 (Women Helpline), and at least 3 trusted contacts on speed dial. Test them periodically.

🏠

Domestic Safety

If facing domestic violence, contact Women Helpline 181. Keep important documents in a safe place accessible to you only.

🔒

Secure Your Home

Use verified door locks, peepholes, and avoid sharing home address publicly. Report suspicious activity to neighbors and police.

👁️

Stay Aware

Avoid using headphones at full volume in public. Stay aware of your surroundings. Trust your instincts — if something feels wrong, leave.

⚖️

Know Your Rights

Under IPC Section 498A, domestic cruelty is punishable. Under POCSO Act, sexual offenses against women have strict penalties. Report without fear.

🧒

Teach Safe Touch

Educate children about body autonomy. Teach them the difference between safe and unsafe touch. Encourage them to speak up without fear.

📞

Childline 1098

Teach children to call 1098 (Childline) if they are in danger or feel unsafe. The call is free and available 24×7.

🏫

School Safety

Know your child's daily school route. Build a network with other parents. Speak to teachers if you notice behavioral changes in your child.

💻

Online Safety

Monitor children's online activity. Use parental controls. Teach them never to share personal information, photos, or location with strangers online.

🚗

Stranger Danger

Teach children to never go anywhere with strangers. Create a family code word for emergencies. Practice "what if" scenarios with them regularly.

❤️

Open Communication

Create a safe space at home where children feel comfortable sharing their experiences and fears without judgment. Listen actively.

🔐

Strong Passwords

Use strong, unique passwords for each account. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts including email and banking apps.

📸

Beware of Sextortion

Never share intimate photos/videos with anyone online. If threatened, call Cyber Crime Helpline 1930. Report immediately — you are the victim, not the criminal.

🎣

Phishing Awareness

Never click suspicious links in SMS or email. Banks never ask for OTPs over phone. If in doubt, call the organization directly using their official number.

👥

Social Media Privacy

Keep your social media profiles private. Avoid sharing real-time location, home address, or daily routine publicly. Review app permissions regularly.

🚫

Cyberbullying

Document all cyberbullying incidents (screenshots). Block the harasser, report to the platform, and file a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930.

🛡️

Secure Devices

Keep your phone and apps updated. Use screen locks. Don't use public Wi-Fi for banking. Install reputable antivirus software on all devices.

🌙

Night Travel

Avoid traveling alone at night. Use verified cab apps, share trip details with family, and sit in the back seat. Note the cab number before boarding.

🚌

Public Transport

Use designated ladies compartments/zones. Stay near the driver/conductor if feeling unsafe. Call 112 if you experience harassment on a bus or train.

🗺️

Plan Your Route

Research your route before traveling to a new area. Download offline maps. Identify police stations, hospitals, and well-lit areas along your route.

👜

Secure Belongings

Keep bags in front of you in crowded places. Don't display expensive items. Carry only essential documents and use digital copies where possible.

✈️

Outstation Travel

Register your travel plans with a trusted person. Carry an Indian ID proof at all times. Know the local police station address of your destination.

📲

Stay Connected

Keep your phone charged. Carry a power bank. Download the SurakshaAI app and set emergency contacts before any long journey.
